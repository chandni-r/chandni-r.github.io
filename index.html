Tactopus,
Inclusive Ed-tech

A play-based, early learning startup I founded during design school in response to accessibility issues I encountered during research.

Tactopus makes playful mixed-reality learning aids that include children with disabilities in the learning process.

Over the past 4 years, it has grown into a team of ten, working with schools, teachers, families and children globally.

Board Game

A game of rotating rings, where players start from the outside, and make their way towards the centre to win. To make a path to the centre, one must rotate the rings, one at a time. With every move, the board changes for the player and opponent alike, disrupting the best laid plans.

assistive technology, text-input, soft-keyboard

Accessible Keyboard

Design of a soft-keyboard that uses the edges of the phone screen as tangible marker, so users with vision loss can type faster. The edges become the reference framework for the fingers, and text input is by swiping the letters in from the edges.
